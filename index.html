Jun, 2024 - Present

Intuit, Software Engineer II, QuickBooks Sales & Invoicing

I work on the QuickBooks Sales & Invoicing platform, one of Intuit's most critical backend services handling over 50,000 transactions per minute and billions in annual payments. My day-to-day sits at the intersection of large-scale distributed systems and real user impact: the work I ship directly affects how small business owners create invoices and get paid.

A big part of my role has been leading the migration of legacy QuickBooks APIs onto a modern Java/Spring Boot microservices stack running on Kubernetes, essentially breaking apart a monolith that's been around for years and giving each domain its own clean, maintainable service. I also introduced a Redis caching layer that cut our worst-case latency by 4x on the hottest read paths, and designed the GraphQL layer that powers the entire Sales surface in the QuickBooks mobile app.

I'm primary on-call for the platform, which has shaped how I think about reliability. When a US-East P0 took down invoice creation for an entire region, I traced the failure to an open circuit breaker within 10 minutes and led the traffic cutover that restored service. The root cause was an unbounded in-memory cache slowly OOM-crashing pods; it took two days to fully understand but only 15 minutes to fix once we had the answer.

Stack: Java, Spring Boot, Kafka, Kubernetes, AWS, Redis, Docker, Splunk
